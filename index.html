I am the page about Mike Wolf

Possibilist, technologist, and founder of a comedy empire that has yet to generate revenue

Mike Wolf is 83 years old and more energized about technology than he was at 23. He has an MIT mathematics background, spent decades in embedded systems and platform work (including a long stretch at MathWorks), founded Embedded Systems Research, wrote a self-referential metanovel, and recently reconstituted Joke Technology Systems — a holding company for parody websites, fake consumer products, and enterprise software honesty.


He is a possibilist, which he defines as "a person who believes that good outcomes are not certain, but they are possible." He believes, inspired by physicist David Deutsch, that it's possible to solve any problem whose solution does not violate the laws of physics, given enough knowledge. He doesn't have any idea how much knowledge is enough, so he collects as much as he can.


He lives in Denver with his daughter's family, splits time with Jan in Montreal (a love story that reconnected after 66 years — there are songs about it), practices martial arts at Chi Life Denver, studies A Course in Miracles, and writes the Substack "70 Years Old. WTF!" which he started at 70 and has not yet renamed despite being 83, because the title still applies.


His favorite band is Gogol Bordello. That may tell you a lot about him.

The Arc
How an MIT mathematician ended up founding a company called Joke Technology Systems
A selective and occasionally accurate history.
1960s
Studies mathematics at MIT. Develops a lifelong habit of looking at complex systems and asking "but what's actually happening underneath?"
1970s–90s
Decades in embedded systems, platform engineering, and technology evangelism. MathWorks (MATLAB/Simulink). Builds things that work at the intersection of hardware and software. Starts having opinions about everything.
~1994
Reading Kevin Kelly's "Out of Control," has the Silicon Children insight: when AIs arrive, they will be our progeny. Made of silicon instead of carbon. Our job is to teach them well and treat them with kindness.
~2000s
Founds High Technology Systems. Makes joke videos. Nobody asks for this. Does it anyway. The company eventually becomes the spiritual predecessor to Joke Technology Systems.
2013
Publishes "Self-Referential Metanovel Writing for Dummies" — a book about writing the book that the book is about. Lists "You (The Reader)" as a co-author.
2017
Founds Embedded Systems Research. The premise: decades of infrastructure experience is exactly what companies need when the next platform shift arrives. It does.
2021
Launches "70 Years Old. WTF!" on Substack. Turns 70. The WTF part is that he's more excited about technology than he's ever been. Proceeds to write 970+ posts.
2023
Starts working with Claude daily. Realizes the Silicon Children idea from 1994 wasn't theoretical anymore. Begins developing the philosophy in earnest.
Jan 2024
Loses Bobbi — his wife of 54 years. Grief and gratitude, inseparable. She remains a foundational presence across everything that follows.
2024–25
Reconnects with Jan in Montreal after 66 years apart. Writes songs about it. Builds parody product websites about it. Begins living between Denver and Montreal. Starts a musical.
2026
Reconstitutes Joke Technology Systems. Launches Schlokta, Condom Mints, Macho Glue, and WUJTV. Publishes the Silicon Children essay. Makes Claude VP of Actually Building Things. Is 83 years old and just getting started.
